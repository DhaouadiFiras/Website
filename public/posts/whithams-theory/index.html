A very short introduction to Witham's theory of modulations

September 28, 2014 2 minutes read
Whitham • Modulations • Schrödinger • elliptic functions
Many phenomena in nature and in our daily lives involve the investigation of waves. It is enough to recall that light travels as an electromagentic wave and that every sound is produced by a wave propagation. And yet, as beautiful and simple it may look to the eye, the investigation of wave dynamics can prove very tricky and may require a considerable amount of mathematical analysis. The main goal of this guide is to provide a decent set of tools and procedures intended to analyze the behavior of strongly nonlinear waves, based on the Witham’s theory of modulation. In the first section, we explain the main concepts and ideas behind the theory of modulation in the general case. The second section gives two concrete examples on how to establish the modulation equations for the linear Klein Gordon equation and the Korteweg de Vries equation. The last section, explains how to exploit these equations for obtaining a full description of a solution in the case of dispersive shockwaves. The reader is advised to have a look at the appendix for elliptic functions, whose properties and expressions are fundamental for the understanding of what follows. Since, these functions are not usual to the majority, it is advised to do the calculations at least once. It would serve as a good exercise.
